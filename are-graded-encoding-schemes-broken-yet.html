A quick overview of attacks on Graded Encoding Schemes.

GGH13

The first candidate graded encoding scheme based on an NTRU-like problem in ideal lattices EC:GarGenHal13.

Follow-Up Constructions

Attacks on GGH13 also apply to these follow-up constructions, which reduce parameter sizes.

  • EC:LanSteSte14
  • AC:ACLL15

BROKEN Key Exchange

Polynomial-time attack in EPRINT:HuJia15

BROKEN Indistinguishability Obfuscation

  • FOCS:GGHRSW13 is broken in polynomial-time in EPRINT:MilSahZha16
  • EC:BGKPS14 is broken in polynomial-time in EPRINT:MilSahZha16
  • EPRINT:AGIS14 is broken in polynomial-time in EPRINT:MilSahZha16
  • EPRINT:BMSZ15 is broken in polynomial-time in EPRINT:MilSahZha16

CLT13

The second candidate graded encoding scheme based on a problem related to the approximate GCD assumption C:CorLepTib13.

BROKEN Follow-Up Construction

C:CorLepTib15 was put forward in response to the attack in EC:CHLRS15, but was broken in polynomial-time for all applications in EPRINT:MinFou15 and EPRINT:CheLeeRyu15.

BROKEN Key Exchange

Polynomial-time attack in EC:CHLRS15.
